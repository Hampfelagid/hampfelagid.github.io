Dr. Lumír Ondřej Hanuš - CZ

Dr. Lumír Ondřej Hanuš is a distinguished analytical chemist and a retired Chief Scientist. He served at Lumir Lab, Asana Bio Group Ltd., and the Institute for Drug Research at the Hebrew University, Jerusalem. Dr. Hanuš completed his M.Sc. in analytical chemistry from Palacký University, Olomouc, in 1972, and earned his Doctor of Natural Sciences in 1974. His research has significantly contributed to the identification of cannabinoid compounds in Cannabis sativa.

Dr. Hanuš's career includes pivotal roles at the Hebrew University, where he was a postdoctoral fellow and later an associate professor. He has conducted extensive research in cannabinoid chemistry and was involved in groundbreaking studies on natural ligands for cannabinoid receptors. His academic journey also includes notable tenures at the University of Mississippi and the National Institutes of Health, Bethesda, USA.

A multi-lingual scholar, Dr. Hanuš's accolades include the Hanuš Medal, Doctor Chemiae Honoris Causa, and the 2015 Lifetime Award from Americans for Safe Access. His memberships span several scientific and mountaineering organizations. Dr. Hanuš remains an influential figure in cannabinoid research, with numerous editorial and advisory roles in prestigious scientific journals and institutions worldwide.
